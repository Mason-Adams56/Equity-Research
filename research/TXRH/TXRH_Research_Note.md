# Texas Roadhouse, Inc. (TXRH) - Research Note

**Date:** July 20, 2026
**Analyst:** Mason
**Rating framework:** Constructive (educational, not a recommendation)

## 1. Business Overview

Texas Roadhouse operates casual dining steakhouses across three concepts: the flagship Texas Roadhouse, Bubba's 33 (sports bar), and Jaggers (fast casual). The company finished Q1 2026 with 822 restaurants worldwide and plans about 35 company-owned openings in 2026. Unlike heavily franchised peers, most restaurants are company-operated, so unit economics flow directly through the income statement. Average weekly sales per restaurant reached $174,151 in Q1, including $25,374 of to-go sales.

## 2. Industry and Competitive Position

Casual dining is a brutal category where most concepts fight declining traffic with discounting. Texas Roadhouse is the standout exception: it wins on value perception (large portions at moderate prices, hand-cut steaks, scratch kitchen) and operator incentives, since managing partners invest in their own restaurant and share in profits. Competitors include Outback (Bloomin' Brands), LongHorn (Darden), and Applebee's, but the traffic gap between TXRH and the category has persisted for years. The moat is culture and execution rather than intellectual property, which is worth interrogating in the thesis.

## 3. Thesis

**Driver 1: Traffic-led growth is the highest quality kind.** Q1 2026 comparable sales rose 7.1%, composed of 4.5% traffic growth and 3.1% pricing. Most restaurant comps today are price-only; TXRH is serving meaningfully more guests, the best mark since Q4 2024. Traffic-led comps compound because they do not exhaust consumer goodwill. Metric to watch: traffic staying positive even if pricing moderates.

**Driver 2: Margin pressure is commodity noise, not a broken model.** Restaurant margin was 16.3%, down only 36 bps despite 6.2% commodity inflation (mostly beef) and 3.8% labor inflation, because sales leverage absorbed the costs. Beef cycles turn; when they do, margin recaptures. Metric to watch: restaurant margin trajectory as beef costs lap.

**Driver 3: A long unit growth runway with proven concepts.** Roughly 5.3% annual unit growth over two years, faster than the sector, plus two younger concepts (Bubba's 33, Jaggers) that extend the runway beyond steakhouse saturation. Store week growth of 5 to 6% is guided for 2026 with about $400 million of capex.

## 4. Recent Results (Q1 2026, 13 weeks ended March 31, 2026)

- Revenue $1.6B, up 12.8%
- Comparable restaurant sales up 7.1% (traffic +4.5%, pricing +3.1%)
- Net income $123.4M, up 8.6%; diluted EPS $1.87 vs $1.70
- Restaurant margin 16.3%, down 36 bps; commodity inflation 6.2%, labor inflation 3.8%
- 822 restaurants; ~35 company-owned openings planned for 2026
- Quarterly dividend raised to $0.75 per share
- Technology investments called out: pay-at-table tablets, digital kitchen displays, new waitlist system

## 5. Valuation

Preferred method: DCF driven by unit growth, average unit volume growth, and restaurant margin, cross-checked against the historical forward P/E range. The model writes itself from three questions: how many restaurants can the three concepts ultimately support, do AUVs keep growing above inflation, and does restaurant margin recapture toward 17%+ when beef normalizes. The stock had rallied 17% over 90 days post-earnings but remained down year to date at the time of the Q1 report, which is the kind of divergence between fundamentals and price action worth investigating. As a mid-cap with lighter sell-side coverage than the mega-caps, this is where independent work can actually be differentiated.

## 6. Risks

1. **Beef inflation persists longer than the cycle suggests.** Commodity inflation of 6.2% is being absorbed by sales leverage; if traffic slows while beef stays high, margins compress from both ends.
2. **Consumer discretionary pullback.** Steak dinners are among the first cuts in a stressed household budget, though management noted no behavior change despite rising gas prices and weak consumer sentiment.
3. **Culture dilution at scale.** The managing partner model is the moat; it gets harder to staff with excellence at 900+ units.
4. **Concept concentration.** The flagship brand still drives the overwhelming majority of profit; Bubba's 33 and Jaggers are unproven at scale.

## 7. Conclusion

Texas Roadhouse is executing the rarest playbook in restaurants: positive traffic, disciplined pricing, and unit growth at the same time, while eating 6% commodity inflation with only 36 bps of margin give-up. The thesis is that traffic-led comps plus an eventual beef cost tailwind produce earnings growth the current multiple undervalues. Watching Q2 for traffic durability and any early sign of beef cost relief.
