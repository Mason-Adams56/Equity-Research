# Caterpillar Inc. (CAT) - Research Note

**Date:** July 20, 2026
**Analyst:** Mason
**Rating framework:** Constructive with valuation discipline (educational, not a recommendation)

## 1. Business Overview

Caterpillar is the world's largest manufacturer of construction and mining equipment, plus engines, turbines, and locomotives, organized across Construction Industries, Resource Industries, and Energy & Transportation, with a captive finance arm. Roughly speaking, Caterpillar sells the machines that build, dig, and power the physical economy, and increasingly the engines that power data centers. Full-year 2025 revenue was $67.6 billion, up 4% from 2024.

## 2. Industry and Competitive Position

Heavy equipment is a scale and dealer-network business. Caterpillar's independent dealer network and installed base drive a growing, higher-margin parts and services annuity that cushions equipment cycles. Primary competitors are Komatsu, Deere (in overlapping categories), and Cummins on the engine side. The newest competitive arena is large reciprocating engines and turbines for data center prime and backup power, where demand currently exceeds industry capacity.

## 3. Thesis

**Driver 1: The backlog has transformed revenue visibility.** Backlog reached a record $63 billion in Q1 2026, up 79% year over year, with all three primary segments contributing. For a company with roughly $70 billion in annual revenue, that is close to a year of committed demand, which is unusual for a cyclical. The market still prices CAT partly as a cycle stock; the backlog argues the earnings floor has risen. Metric to watch: book-to-bill and backlog conversion each quarter.

**Driver 2: Data center power is a structural, not cyclical, demand source.** Power generation sales to users jumped 32% in Q1, and management announced expansion of large reciprocating engine capacity to nearly three times 2024 levels. A five-year framework agreement with PROPWR for up to 2.1 gigawatts of power generation assets is one visible example. If AI data center buildout continues, Energy & Transportation becomes a growth segment attached to a cyclical company. Metric to watch: Power & Energy segment margin holding above 20%.

**Driver 3: Tariffs are a known, bounded headwind the market may be over-discounting.** Q1 tariff costs of roughly $600 million came in below the $800 million management had estimated, with full-year guidance of $2.2 to $2.4 billion. Bounded costs against 22% revenue growth are manageable; the risk is if they deepen.

## 4. Recent Results (Q1 2026, reported April 30, 2026)

- Sales and revenues $17.4B, up 22% vs $14.2B in Q1 2025
- Adjusted EPS $5.54 vs $4.25 prior year (reported $5.47), a roughly 19% beat vs consensus near $4.62
- Record backlog $63B, up $28B or 79% YoY
- Tariff costs ~$600M in Q1; full-year estimate $2.2B to $2.4B
- Resource Industries margin 10.0%, down about 700 bps YoY, the soft spot in the print
- Deployed $5.7B to shareholders including an approximately $4.5B accelerated share repurchase
- Guidance: low double digit full-year 2026 revenue growth; ME&T free cash flow expected above 2025's ~$9.5B

## 5. Valuation

Preferred method: DCF on Machinery, Energy & Transportation free cash flow with a mid-cycle margin assumption, cross-checked against historical P/E ranges. The stock has re-rated on the data center power story, so the question is how much structural growth is already paid for. Key model choices: how fast the $63B backlog converts, whether Resource Industries margin recovers above 13%, and whether tariff costs land at the low or high end of the $2.2 to $2.4B range. Run a scenario where backlog conversion slips two quarters and margins stay compressed; if the downside from today's price is tolerable there, the risk-reward is attractive.

## 6. Risks

1. **Tariff escalation.** Guidance embeds $2.2 to $2.4B; policy is outside management's control and the Q1 beat was partly a tariff timing benefit.
2. **Resource Industries margin stays compressed.** Down 700 bps in Q1. If Q2 does not show recovery, cost headwinds run deeper than guided.
3. **Data center power demand air pocket.** Capacity is being tripled into a demand forecast; if AI infrastructure spending pauses, that capacity becomes a fixed cost problem.
4. **Cyclicality has not been repealed.** Construction and mining demand still follow commodity prices and rates.

## 7. Conclusion

Caterpillar's Q1 was the strongest evidence yet that the company is becoming part industrial cyclical, part power infrastructure play. The $63 billion backlog and tripling of engine capacity change the shape of the business. I am constructive on fundamentals but disciplined on entry price, since the stock already reflects much of the story. Watching Resource Industries margin at Q2 earnings (expected August 5, 2026) as the key tell on whether the re-rating holds.
