# Equity Research Portfolio

Independent equity research written by Mason, Finance major (Accounting minor) at Susquehanna University, Class of 2028.

Each company folder contains a written research note and a supporting valuation model. All research follows the same process: read the filings, build the model, form a thesis, and document the risks that would break it. Every note is dated so calls can be judged against what was knowable at the time.

## Process

1. **Primary sources first.** 10-K, 10-Q, earnings releases, and call transcripts from SEC EDGAR and company investor relations pages.
2. **Model the business.** Revenue drivers, margin structure, and free cash flow, built in Excel (see [templates](templates/)).
3. **Write the thesis.** Two to three drivers I believe the market underweights, plus explicit risks and what would make me wrong.
4. **Value it.** DCF where the business supports it, with multiples as a sanity check. Banks are valued on return on tangible common equity versus price to tangible book.
